Systems Team Builder

A simple leadership page for one-on-ones, team discussions, and early team development. It stores data in your browser using localStorage, so it works as a single HTML file on your computer or a server.

Question Bank

These are aimed at surfacing strengths, working style, aspirations, and what each person wants from the Systems Team and McKinney Public Library more broadly.

1: Low-pressure icebreakers for one-on-ones or first team meetings
  1. What is one thing people here might not know about you yet?
  2. What kind of work day leaves you feeling like, “That was a good day”?
  3. What is something you tend to enjoy more than most people do at work?
  4. What is a skill you have that does not always show up in your job description?
  5. What is one tool, habit, or routine that helps you stay organized?
  6. What kind of tasks give you energy, and which ones drain it?
  7. What is one work accomplishment you are proud of from the last year?
  8. What helps you feel welcomed into a new group?
2: Questions to identify strengths, talents, and working style
  1. What part of your current work comes most naturally to you?
  2. When coworkers come to you for help, what do they usually ask you about?
  3. Do you prefer being the person who starts new things, improves existing things, or keeps things steady and reliable?
  4. What kind of problems do you most enjoy solving?
  5. How do you like to learn something new: hands-on, reading, watching, shadowing, or talking it through?
  6. How do you prefer to receive feedback?
  7. When you are under pressure, what support helps the most?
  8. What does good teamwork look like to you?
3: Questions about goals, growth, and career direction
  1. What would you like to be better at six months from now?
  2. Are there projects, systems, or responsibilities you want more exposure to?
  3. What professional skills would you like to build while you are at MPL?
  4. What kind of support or opportunities would help you grow here?
  5. Do you see yourself wanting to deepen your expertise, broaden your skills, or move toward leadership?
  6. What does success in your role look like to you?
  7. What kinds of work would you like to be trusted with more often?
  8. What would make this team a place where you want to stay and grow?
4: Questions about the team and MPL as a workplace
  1. What do you hope this new Systems Team will be known for?
  2. What should this team do really well for staff and patrons?
  3. What gets in the way of doing great work in a library systems environment?
  4. What is one thing MPL does well that we should protect?
  5. What is one thing MPL could improve that would help staff do better work?
  6. How can this team build trust with the rest of the library?
  7. What kind of communication from leadership is most helpful to you?
  8. What would help you feel connected to MPL’s bigger mission?
5: Good team discussion prompts
  1. What do we want people to experience when they interact with the Systems Team?
  2. What does strong internal customer service look like for us?
  3. What expectations should we set for communication, response time, and follow-through?
  4. What are our team norms around documentation and knowledge sharing?
  5. How do we want to handle mistakes, handoffs, and asking for help?
  6. What strengths do we already have on this team, and where are our gaps?
  7. What is one thing each person could teach the rest of the team?
  8. What should we commit to in our first 90 days as a team?
6: Optional deeper one-on-one questions for later
  1. What kind of leadership brings out your best work?
  2. What is something previous supervisors did that really helped you succeed?
  3. What is something you wish managers understood better about your role?
  4. What kind of recognition feels meaningful to you?
  5. What early warning signs tell you that you are overloaded or getting frustrated?
  6. What do you want me to know about how you work before we get too far into this?
