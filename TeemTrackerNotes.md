## Ice Breaker & Discovery Questions

### Skills & Strengths
1. What's a task at work where you feel most "in your element"? *(Both)*
2. What's something you know how to do that most people would be surprised by? *(Group)*
3. If our team was stuck on a tough problem, what role would you naturally fall into — researcher, idea generator, organizer, executor? *(Both)*
4. What tools, systems, or workflows have you become the go-to person for — either here or at a previous job? *(1-on-1)*
5. Is there a project or task type you've always wanted to take the lead on? *(1-on-1)*

### Professional Goals
6. Where do you hope to be professionally in 2–3 years? *(1-on-1)*
7. What's one skill you've been wanting to develop but haven't had the chance to yet? *(1-on-1)*
8. Is there a type of project or responsibility you've never had but would love to try? *(1-on-1)*
9. What does a personal "win" look like for you on this team — what would make you feel like it was worth being here? *(1-on-1)*
10. If you could add one thing to your resume by the end of the year, what would it be? *(1-on-1)*

### Team & MPL Culture
11. What made you want to work at a public library specifically? *(Both)*
12. What's something a previous team or workplace did really well that you'd love to bring here? *(Both)*
13. How do you prefer to receive feedback — direct and quick, or with context and a lead-up conversation? *(1-on-1)*
14. What's one thing that makes you feel genuinely valued at work? *(1-on-1)*
15. What do you think makes a library system team different from other teams at an organization like this? *(Group)*

### Ice Breakers — Lighter, Great for Group Settings
16. What's a book, show, podcast, or hobby you'd recommend to everyone on this team? *(Group)*

19. What's the most interesting patron interaction or library moment you've witnessed? *(Group)*
20. If this team had a name — like a sports team or a band — what would you call us? *(Group)*

## 1-on-1 Meeting Structure

Each meeting follows a four-part, ~30-minute framework:

1. **Rapport (5 min)** — Human connection before business. Ask something personal and genuinely listen.
2. **Recent Work (10 min)** — Specific praise and a progress check. Lead with something you appreciated.
3. **Deep Dive (10 min)** — Focus on a theme: growth, feedback, culture, well-being, or performance.
4. **Manager Support (5 min)** — Close by asking how *you* can help *them*.

**The Golden Rule:** This is their meeting, not yours. Aim for an 80/20 split — they talk 80% of the time.

**Not a 1-on-1:** Status updates, project check-ins, or one-way communication.

**Is a 1-on-1:** Coaching, career development, trust-building, and honest dialogue.

### Tough Conversation Frameworks

**Constructive Feedback — SBI Model**
- *Situation:* "On Tuesday..."
- *Behavior:* "...you cut someone off..."
- *Impact:* "...which derailed the discussion."
Keep it factual and specific, never personal.

**Disengagement**
Acknowledge openly and without blame: *"I've noticed you've seemed quieter lately — is everything okay?"* Opening the door gently is more effective than waiting for them to come to you.

**Receiving Upward Feedback**
When you ask what you could do better as a manager, your only job is to truly listen. Say *"Thank you — I appreciate you telling me that."* Never explain or get defensive in the moment.

## Tracker Tool — Feature Summary

The HTML tracker page contains four tabs:

**Team Overview** — Card view of all four team members with tenure indicators, field completion progress, and quick-access previews of skills and goals. Members can be added at any time.

**Member Detail Pages** — Each member profile has four sections: Basic Info, Skills & Strengths, Professional Goals, and Working Style & Culture Fit — plus a free-form notes area for 1-on-1 observations, team session notes, and action items.

**Ice Breaker Questions** — All 20 questions organized by category, each tagged as Group, 1-on-1, or Both.

**1-on-1 Meetings** — Three sub-tabs:
- *Conduct Meeting:* Select from 25 templates across 5 themes; team member names auto-populate from the tracker; four color-coded question blocks for note-taking; generates a formatted summary with copy and print options; save to history.
- *Guide & Best Practices:* The Golden Rule, four-part structure, 80/20 tip, and the three tough-conversation frameworks.
- *Meeting History:* All saved meetings by employee and date, viewable in a modal, individually deletable.

**All data saves to browser local storage** — no server, login, or internet connection required once the file is downloaded.
