Meeting Summary

The "Why" — The Golden Rule

This is their meeting, not yours. Your job is to listen, guide, and support — not to report out or manage tasks.

NOT a 1-on-1: Status updates, project check-ins, or one-way communication from manager to employee.
IS a 1-on-1: Coaching, career development, trust-building, and genuine two-way dialogue.

The "How" — Four-Part Structure

Each template follows this ~30-minute framework:

  1. Rapport (5 min): Human connection before business.
  2. Recent Work (10 min): Specific praise and a progress check.
  3. Deep Dive (10 min): Focus on a theme — growth, culture, feedback, well-being.
  4. Manager Support (5 min): Close by asking how you can help them.
Pro tip: Aim for an 80/20 split. The employee should be talking 80% of the time. Ask open questions and sit with silences.

Navigating Tough Conversations

Constructive Feedback

Use SBI: Situation ("On Tuesday..."), Behavior ("...you cut someone off..."), Impact ("...which derailed the discussion"). Keep it factual and specific — never personal.

Signs of Disengagement

Acknowledge it openly and without blame: "I've noticed you've seemed quieter lately — is everything okay?" Opening the door gently is far more effective than waiting for them to come to you.

Receiving Upward Feedback

When you ask what you could do better as a manager, your only job is to truly listen. Say "Thank you — I appreciate you telling me that." Never explain or get defensive in the moment.
