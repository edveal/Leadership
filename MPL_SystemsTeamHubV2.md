# MPL Systems Team Hub v2 — Usage Notes

## Overview

The **MPL Systems Team Hub v2** is a lightweight leadership tool designed to support:

* 1-on-1 conversations
* Team development and alignment
* Skill and talent identification
* Ongoing performance and growth tracking
* Documentation of commitments and follow-up

All data is stored locally in your browser (localStorage), with export/import available for backup.

---

## Core Philosophy (How to Use This Well)

* This is **not a tracking tool for tasks** — it’s a tool for people
* 1-on-1s are **their meeting, not yours**
* Aim for **80/20 talk time** (they talk more than you)
* Focus on:

  * strengths
  * growth
  * clarity
  * support

---

## Main Sections

### 1. Team Profiles

This is your **long-term memory** for each employee.

Use it to capture:

* **Strengths / Skills**
* **Hidden talents**
* **Professional goals**
* **Motivators**
* **Support needs**
* **General notes**
* **Follow-up commitments**

#### How to Use

* Fill this out over the first 2–3 one-on-ones
* Update periodically (not every meeting)
* Look for patterns across staff

#### Tip

Don’t try to fill everything in at once. Let it emerge naturally.

---

### 2. 1-on-1 Workspace

This is your **active meeting tool**.

It uses structured templates based on:

* Rapport
* Recent Work
* Deep Dive
* Manager Support

#### Workflow

1. Select a template
2. Enter employee name
3. Capture notes during conversation
4. Generate summary
5. Save to history

#### When to Use Which Templates

* **New staff (first 90 days)**
  → Growth, onboarding, clarity templates

* **Established staff**
  → Feedback, team dynamics, innovation

* **Struggling / disengaged**
  → Well-being, workload, support templates

---

### 3. Meeting History

This is your **record of conversations**.

Each saved meeting includes:

* Employee name
* Template used
* Date
* Full notes

#### How to Use

* Review before next 1-on-1
* Look for:

  * repeated blockers
  * stalled goals
  * missed commitments

---

### 4. Team Comparison View

This is your **systems-level leadership view**.

Use it to compare across staff:

* Skills
* Goals
* Motivators
* Support needs

#### What to Look For

* Skill gaps across the team
* Overlap (too many people doing the same thing)
* Leadership potential
* Training opportunities

---

### 5. Print / Summary Tools

Use this for:

* Performance discussions
* Self-review prep
* Leadership updates
* Your own reflection

---

## Recommended Cadence

### First 30–60 Days

* Focus on:

  * rapport
  * strengths
  * understanding motivations
* Use lighter templates
* Start building profiles

---

### Ongoing (Monthly Rhythm)

**Each 1-on-1:**

* Quick rapport check
* Review recent work
* One focused deep dive
* Clear next steps

**Every 4–6 weeks:**

* Revisit goals
* Update profile notes

---

### Quarterly

* Review all team profiles together
* Use comparison view to:

  * rebalance work
  * identify growth paths
  * plan training

---

## Practical Tips (From Experience)

### 1. Don’t Over-Document

You’re not writing a report. Capture:

* key phrases
* patterns
* commitments

---

### 2. Listen for Signals

Pay attention to:

* “I like when…” → motivators
* “I struggle with…” → support needs
* “I wish I could…” → growth direction

---

### 3. Use It Before the Meeting

Quick prep (2–3 minutes):

* glance at last notes
* check commitments
* pick the right template

---

### 4. Close Every Meeting Clearly

Always end with:

* what they will do
* what you will do

Write it down.

---

### 5. Use It to Build the Team, Not Just Individuals

Look across profiles and ask:

* Where are we strong?
* Where are we thin?
* Who can teach what?
* Where do I need to step in?

---

## Data & Backup Notes

* Data is stored in your browser
* It will **not sync across devices**
* Always:

  * Export JSON periodically
  * Save a backup copy
